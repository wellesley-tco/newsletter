  • Place Feature - 228 Memorial Peace Park (Taipei, Taiwan)
    • This memorial park located near the Presidential Office Building was dedicated to all the victims of a period known as White Terror. Post WWII, the KMT ruled the island with an iron fist to prevent social uprisings. The period of martial law lasted for 38 years and estimates of casualties ranged from 10,000-30,000+. More details here.
    • Prior to the digital age when communication was not as convenient, the 228 Memorial Peace Park was also an important safe place for the gay community to gather and build their gender identity. The park was also known as "公司“ (or "company") among the community, a metaphor that hints at "carding in and out" as part of one's daily routine.
